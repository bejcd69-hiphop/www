The Bihać Hip Hop Scene

The Bihać Hip Hop scene began developing in the late 1970s and continued through the 1980s, influenced by American Hip Hop and the local youth culture. Bihać emerged as one of the earliest and most active Hip Hop centers in Bosnia and Herzegovina, particularly in the Nova Četvrt (New Quart) and Harmani/Centre neighborhoods.

Late 1970s – Early 1980s: Foundations

Foundations

Late 1970s — Early listening

Beytz D begins listening to Break Beat and Funk from labels like Sugar Hill Records and Enjoy Records—before the term “Hip Hop” was widely coined.

Foundations

Early 1980s — First B‑Boys & crews

Jasko Pecenković (Pečeni) becomes the first B‑Boy in Nova Četvrt. Beytz D joins him to form Brejkeri sa Nove Četvrti (New Quart Breakers).

  • 1982–1990: Performances at city schools (Avnoj, USAOJ, Technical High School) and weekend discos (Borići dorm in New Quart, Brklja, Žegar, Sokolac).
  • Rivals: Harmani B‑Boys (Harmani Breakers) from Harmani/Centre local communities.
  • Notable B‑Boys: Brki, Pico Copy, Hary, Jakanni (RIP), Dejo, Grba, MCA, Mera MC, MC Burzoti, Pečeni, Dicka, Dule, Bestak, Dino, Drugi Dino, Boban, Pipi, and others.
  • Where music was bought: Department store Beograd; RIZ record & cassette store.

1984–1990: Golden Age — Broadcast, Cinema & Rise

Radio

1984 — Tapes & radio shows

Beytz D discovers Dona Muzički Vlak (Dona Music Train, known locally as Rap Attack) on Radio Velika Gorica hosted by Slavin Balen, recording and distributing tapes across Bihać.

Radio

1986 — Electrofunk Premiere → Rap Attack

Beytz D finds Electrofunk Premiere on Radio 101 (Croatia), encouraging Monday-night listening for fresh Hip Hop; a year later the show is branded Rap Attack.

Also influential: Po vašem izboru (Radio Zagreb 1) and Rock Express (Radio Zagreb 2, hosted by Ante Batinović).

Cinema

Mid–late 1980s — Hip Hop films in Bihać cinemas

Screenings of Wild Style, Breakin', Breakin' 2: Electric Boogaloo, Beat Street, and Krush Groove shape B‑Boying, DJ techniques, Hip Hop fashion, and aerosol/graffiti art in the city.

Radio

Local broadcast hub — Radio Club Bihać

From 1983 onward, DJ Vuki and DJ Ido (MC Fresh Mix) use the local Bihać Radio Club to distribute and broadcast Hip Hop in the city.

Key Figures

Figure

DJ Ido (MC Fresh Mix)

Early Hip Hop pioneer who contributed to B‑Boying, DJing, MCing, and graffiti in Bihać; worked with DJ Vuki, Beytz D, “Mali” Rade, Deyan, Meshan, and Maska. Chronicled the scene and bridged Electroshock Crew → Screw Boys to younger generations.

“Regardless [of the] war in Bosnia the true HIP HOP Spirit has survived in Bihać. The young, true HIP HOP warriors have picked up it where the Screw Boys had to stop.”
Figure

DJ Vuki — Pioneer

A pioneering Hip Hop DJ and one of the first aerosol/graffiti artists from Bihać; played a key role in the formative 1980s era.

  • Co‑founded Electroshock Crew with MC Fresh Mix, Mister B, and MCA; after Beytz D joined, the group became Screw Boys.
  • Quote (DJ Ido): “The first name of the crew was ELECTROSHOCK (DJ Vuki, MC Fresh Mix, Mister B and MCA). When the living legend Beytz D joined the crew, the crew changed name to SCREW BOYS.”
  • Main DJ at the first large‑scale Hip Hop event in Bihać, Club Azra (Park Hotel), 1989.

Legacy

Remembered as one of the key architects of Bihać’s earliest Hip Hop movement, paving the way for later generations of DJs, MCs, and aerosol/graffiti artists.

1985–1986: Electroshock Crew → Screw Boys

Crew

Electroshock Crew (1985–1986)

Members: DJ Vuki, DJ Ido (MC Fresh Mix), Mister B, MCA. After Beytz D joined, the crew was renamed Screw Boys.

1987–1990: Scene Expansion & Venues

Venue

“Rupa” (The Hole)

The key underground club located in the basement of the Bihać municipal building, active from 1987–1990. Hosted battles, DJ/MC performances, graffiti sessions, and fashion shows.

1989: First Major Hip Hop Event

Event

Club Azra, Park Hotel — 1989

Organized by Screw Boys; hundreds attended. Full organization included flyers, DJ performances, and curated music.

Screw Boys — Music & Breakup

Crew

The group produced several tracks (unreleased). Their final internal hit, “Night Killer”—inspired by Colors—featured key contributions from Dejan Pavlović and Polar ICE. The crew disbanded during the Yugoslav Wars (1992–1995).
